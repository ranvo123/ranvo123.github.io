# How to Print Your Wall Art

Thank you for your purchase! Your files are **SVG vector art**, which means they
stay perfectly sharp at *any* size — from a 5×7" desk frame to a huge poster.

## Quickest path: print at home or a print shop

1. **Open the SVG** in a web browser (just double-click it) — it will display the art.
2. To get a **JPG/PNG** for a photo-print service, open the SVG in **Canva**
   (upload it), **Inkscape** (free), Illustrator, Figma, or use any free
   "SVG to JPG" converter, then export at **300 DPI**.
3. Upload the exported file to any print service: your home printer,
   **Printful, Prodigy, Walgreens, Staples, or a local print shop.**

## Recommended sizes (this art is a 4:5 ratio)

4:5 is the most popular wall-art ratio and frames are easy to find.

| Frame size | Export pixels @ 300 DPI |
|------------|-------------------------|
| 4 × 5 in | 1200 × 1500 |
| 8 × 10 in | 2400 × 3000 |
| 11 × 14 in* | 3300 × 4200 |
| 16 × 20 in | 4800 × 6000 |
| A4 / A3 | scale to fit, center |

\*11×14 is 4:5.04 — near-identical; add a hair of matting and it fits perfectly.

Because the art is vector, **you can also change the ratio** (e.g. to 2:3 for
common poster frames) by adjusting the canvas in Canva/Illustrator without losing quality.

## Tips for the best result

- Print on **matte or satin photo paper**, 200gsm+ for a gallery feel.
- Use the **"fit / actual size"** setting, not "shrink to fit," to keep proportions.
- For a gallery wall, print the whole set in the same size and frame style.
